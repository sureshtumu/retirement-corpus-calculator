Retirement Corpus — The Magic Number

Every rupee of income at your starting age is split into expense and accumulation lots. Lots inflate with a flat index; accumulation compounds until retirement; the corpus then funds retirement expenses. Change any number — everything recalculates instantly.

Assumptions

gets whatever annuity & FD don't take
How it computes. Income lots inflate each year by the index. Accumulation lots are monthly amounts (₹5/month → ₹60/year, as in your note), added yearly and compounded at the accumulation return until retirement. Required corpus is the amount at retirement that exactly funds each year's inflated retirement expense (expense ratio applied) with the remainder earning the post-retirement return, ending at zero at the plan-until age. Salary grows at its own rate; savings ride on the salary and the retirement lifestyle is anchored to the last-drawn salary, then inflates at price inflation (healthcare at its own rate). At retirement the corpus deploys into buckets: the annuity share is locked for life (income only, principal to nominee), and withdrawals come from FD first, equity last; the magic number is found by search — the smallest corpus that survives the plan under the same bucket policy, after pension or rent income. When a loan or goal with a year-slider ends before retirement, its freed share automatically flows into a matching "Freed when … ends" savings tranche from that very year — shorten an EMI and watch the corpus respond. Edit or Remove the freed tranche to redirect less than 100%. Under every category name the grey line shows the exact span the formula uses — by default Education Loan runs 15 yrs (25→40), House Loan EMI 30 yrs (25→55), Child Education & Marriage 35 yrs (25→60) — and it updates the moment you change From / Till / retirement ages.
